Ethics & Sourcing

Made in Bangladesh. Proudly.

Most brands hide where their clothes come from. We'd rather introduce you.

The Factory

Gazipur, Bangladesh.

Our factory sits just north of Dhaka, in the industrial district of Gazipur. It was purpose-built in 2016, three years after the Rana Plaza tragedy, to the highest standards the Bangladesh Accord could certify.

It's a family-run operation, with an all-female pattern-making team and a long-standing reputation for technical knits. We chose it not because it was cheap, but because it was good — and because its management shared our expectations about what a healthy factory should feel like.

Our Certifications

Transparent Pricing

What your money pays for.

A single Lumen Top costs you $62 CAD. Here's where each dollar goes. We believe in doing this in public.

Fabric & trims $9.50
Factory labour (above market) $6.80
Quality control & inspection $1.40
Freight, duties, brokerage $4.20
Photography, design, website $5.10
Fulfillment & packaging $4.50
Payment processing $1.80
Marketing $3.70
Total cost $37.00
Margin (reinvested into the label) $25.00

If a competitor sells the same product for $89 CAD, what you're paying for is a brand's wholesale markup — not better fabric, better labour, or a better factory. We chose to do the opposite: sell direct, share the numbers, and invest the margin back into the label's next capsule.

Our Commitments

What we promise.

We pay above market.

Our factory wages are benchmarked 15–20% above the regional ready-made-garment average. Documented in our annual transparency report.

We keep dye lots short.

Smaller runs mean less overstock, less waste, and tighter quality. We'd rather sell out than discount.

We take returns seriously.

Every returned piece is inspected. Gently used pieces are donated to Hamilton-area nursing programs, not landfilled.

We publish the numbers.

Once a year, we publish a transparency report: wages, audits, sourcing, margin, donations. No asterisks.
